Description

A strategic product teardown of Abridge, a leading health AI company, analyzing its evolution from clinical documentation tool to how it become  a revenue engine.

Feature prioritization using Kano for AI

Key risks and market timing
A 90-day execution plan as a Product Manager

The real buyer is CFO, not clinician
Documentation is a commodity within 18–24 months
The winning strategy is moving upstream into revenue cycle + decision support

Ability to connect product → business outcomes
Structured frameworks (JTBD, Kano)
Strong POV on market + competition
Execution-oriented thinking (90-day plan)
